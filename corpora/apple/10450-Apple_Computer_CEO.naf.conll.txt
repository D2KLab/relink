Apple Computer CEO Steve Jobs gives opening keynote to WWDC 2005
June 6 , 2005
Apple Computer CEO and co-founder Steve Jobs gave his annual opening keynote to the World Wide Developers Conference ( WWDC ) at Moscone Center in San Francisco , California on Monday .
He announced a seamless integration of podcasting with iTunes , and , to the amazement of many , that future Apple Macintosh computers will feature Intel processors .
Jobs welcomed everyone to the event and announced that there were 3,800 attendees .
There are 500 Apple engineers at what is their largest developers conference in the past decade .
There are over 500,000 developers of Apple programs ( apps ) .
Jobs said that Apple has 109 retail stores , with one million visitors per week , creating over $ 500 million yearly in revenue .
The Mac market share is up 82 % from 9 months ago .
Jobs then moved on to talk about a very anticipated announcement - Podcasting .
